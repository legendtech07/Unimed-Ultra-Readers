Macromolecules Overview

Carbohydrates

Function as energy-storage molecules and structural elements. Composed of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen in a 1:2:1 ratio.

  • Monosaccharides: Simple sugars like glucose
  • Disaccharides: Two sugar units joined
  • Polysaccharides: Many sugar units (starch, cellulose)

Lipids

Insoluble in water. Functions include energy storage, membrane components, and cell signaling.

  • Fats: Triglycerides composed of glycerol and fatty acids
  • Phospholipids: Form cell membranes
  • Steroids: Cholesterol, hormones like testosterone

Proteins

Polymers of amino acids that execute cellular tasks directed by genetic information.

  • Functions: Enzymes, defense, transport, structure, motion, regulation, storage
  • Structure: 20 different amino acids in specific sequences

Nucleic Acids

The principal informational molecules of the cell.

  • DNA: Stores genetic information
  • RNA: Copies of DNA used for protein synthesis
  • Structure: Polymers of nucleotides

Polymer Formation

Macromolecules are polymers formed by linking monomers through:

  • Dehydration Synthesis: Removal of water to form bonds
  • Hydrolysis: Addition of water to break bonds
