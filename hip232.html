OSTEOLOGY OF THE HIP BONE

MBBS/BDS Gross Anatomy Lecture - Dr. Gideon Oluwatunase

Lecture Overview

The hip bone (os coxae) is an irregularly shaped, bilateral bone of the bony pelvis. It consists of three fused bones: the ilium, ischium, and pubis. This quiz will test your knowledge of hip bone osteology including its structures, landmarks, and clinical applications.

Key Topics Covered:

  • Three components of hip bone
  • Acetabulum anatomy
  • Ilium, ischium, and pubis structures
  • Obturator foramen
  • Applied anatomy (bone marrow biopsy)
